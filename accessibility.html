Accessibility Statement

KidVibers is built for every kid — that includes kids and families using assistive technology.

Last updated July 2026

Our commitment

We aim for KidVibers to be usable by everyone, regardless of ability, and we're working toward conformance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. Accessibility is an ongoing effort, not a one-time checkbox — we keep improving it as the product grows.

What we've built in

  • Semantic HTML structure and labeled form fields throughout the site
  • Keyboard-navigable menus, buttons, and forms
  • Color choices designed with contrast in mind for readability
  • Text alternatives for icons and key visual indicators
  • A responsive layout that works on phones, tablets, and desktops without loss of function
  • Bilingual support (English/Spanish) on the homepage and kid dashboard

Known limitations

We're not perfect yet. Some areas of the product — particularly Vibe Studio's visual template editor — are more visual by nature and may not be fully screen-reader friendly today. We're actively working through these as we continue building.

Feedback & reporting an issue

If you or your child run into an accessibility barrier anywhere on KidVibers, please tell us — we treat these reports seriously and prioritize fixes. Email support@kidvibers.com with:

  • The page or feature where you ran into trouble
  • What assistive technology you were using (screen reader, switch device, etc.), if any
  • What you expected to happen vs. what actually happened

We'll get back to you and let you know what we're doing about it.

For schools & libraries

If your program has specific accessibility requirements (e.g. for an IEP/504 accommodation, or a public-facility ADA requirement), reach out — we're happy to discuss what KidVibers supports today and prioritize what would help most.
