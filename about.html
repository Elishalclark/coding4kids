Our Story

We're on a mission to make coding feel like play for every kid — safely, and for free at schools and libraries.

Why we built KidVibers

Most "learn to code" apps for kids feel like homework — walls of confusing text, syntax errors, and quitting before the fun starts. We thought: what if kids could just make cool stuff and feel like creators from minute one?

That's KidVibers. Kids tell their buddy Byte what they want to build — a website about their dog, a birthday card, a fan page for their favorite game — and they make something real they're proud to share.

What is "vibe coding"?

Vibe coding is the future of creating on the web. Instead of memorizing syntax, kids start from templates and make them their own — changing words, colors, and pictures until it's theirs. They learn how the web works by building, not by being lectured.

It's the difference between reading about music and actually picking up an instrument. Kids build first, and the confidence follows.

Made for schools & libraries

We built KidVibers to be the easiest coding program a library or school could run. No ads, no data selling, drop-in sessions where kids join with just a nickname, and safety features that make approval simple. A volunteer who's never written a line of code can help run a whole session.
