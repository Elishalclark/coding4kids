🎟️ How a session works

  • Librarian starts a session from the dashboard — gets a join code + printable QR flyer.
  • Kids scan the code, pick a nickname, and start coding — no account, email, or app install needed.
  • Kids build real projects (web pages, cards, games) using "vibe coding" — describing what they want rather than typing raw syntax.
  • Guest accounts and their data auto-delete within 24 hours unless a parent chooses to save the account.
  • Session ends with a printable recap: kids joined, creations built, session length — great for program reporting.

🛡️ Safety & privacy, built in

  • No ads, ever. No selling or sharing of student data.
  • Guest sessions collect nothing but a nickname.
  • Concerning language typed anywhere is flagged, with an instant crisis-line resource shown to the kid.
  • A signable Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available for your library or district.
  • Real, hourly automated uptime monitoring — see kidvibers.com/status.

💻 What kids actually do

  • Vibe Studio: build real, working web pages and cards by describing what they want — customizing text, colors, and images, no syntax required to get started.
  • 293 structured lessons across 25 progressively harder worlds, teaching real coding concepts (variables, loops, logic, functions) in short, game-like bites.
  • 12 arcade-style practice games (debugging, speed challenges, logic puzzles) that reinforce lesson concepts without feeling like homework.
  • Byte, a purpose-built help buddy for hints and definitions — not a general AI chatbot, and never connected to the open internet, by design.

📋 What we need from you

  • A room with tablets, Chromebooks, or laptops (anything with a modern browser) and wifi.
  • One staff member or volunteer to run the session — no coding knowledge required.
  • A date! Setup takes about 5 minutes on the day.
