Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 31, 2026

KidVibers.com is built for children, so protecting privacy is at the heart of what we do. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the rights parents have. We follow the spirit of children's privacy laws like COPPA (the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act).

1. What we collect 2. How we use it 3. What we never do 4. Children & COPPA 5. Parent rights 6. Sharing 7. Cookies & storage 8. Third parties 9. Security 10. Keeping & deleting data 11. Changes 12. Contact

1. Information We Collect

We collect as little as possible. For a child account, that is:

  • A first name (or nickname) and a chosen username;
  • A password (stored only as a secure, hashed value — never in plain text);
  • An age range (e.g. 8–10);
  • A parent's email address (to connect a parent and send account messages);
  • Learning data: lessons completed, quiz/boss-battle results, level, streak, tokens, and avatar choices.

For a parent account, we collect a name, username, password (hashed), and email address.

2. How We Use Information

  • To run the learning experience and save progress so kids can continue where they left off;
  • To award tokens and let kids customize their avatar;
  • To connect a parent to their child's account and send account-related messages (for example, when a child asks a parent to upgrade);
  • To keep the platform safe and working properly.

3. What We Never Do

🚫 We do not show ads, we do not sell or rent personal information, and we do not use children's data for behavioral advertising. There is no private messaging between users.

4. Children & COPPA

Because KidVibers is intended for children, we require verifiable parental consent before a child under 13 can use the account. When an under-13 child signs up, the account is held in a "pending" state and cannot be used until a parent/guardian approves it — by creating and linking their own Family account, or by confirming a two-step ("email-plus") consent link we send to the parent's email. For classroom use, a teacher or school may provide consent on parents' behalf for educational purposes. We record each consent (who approved, how, and when) and collect only the information needed to provide the learning service.

A parent or guardian can review, download, or delete their child's data, sign the child out, or withdraw consent at any time from their dashboard.

5. Parent & Guardian Rights

  • Review: see your family's plan and your child's progress from the Family Dashboard.
  • Control: add kids to your family and sign them out of their devices.
  • Correct or delete: contact us to update or delete your child's information.
  • Withdraw consent: you may close the account, which removes it from active use.

6. How Information Is Shared

We don't sell personal information. We only share it with trusted service providers that help us run the platform (for example, hosting), and only as needed to provide the service, or when required by law to keep someone safe.

7. Cookies & Local Storage

We use your device's local storage to keep you signed in (a login token) and to remember small things like your daily streak. We do not use third-party advertising or tracking cookies.

8. Third-Party Services

To show a parent-invite QR code, we generate a QR image using a third-party image service. The data placed in the QR code is the parent-signup link for your child's invite. We don't share other personal details with that service.

9. How We Protect Information

Passwords are stored using strong one-way hashing (PBKDF2 with a unique salt) — we can't see them. Access to assessment answer keys is kept on the server and never sent to the browser. Login sessions are token-based and can be revoked (for example, when a parent signs a kid out).

10. Keeping & Deleting Data

We keep account information and progress so kids don't lose their work — registered accounts are retained until deletion is requested. To delete an account and its associated data, a parent or account owner can contact us at the address below.

11. Changes to This Policy

If we update this policy, we'll change the date above and, for significant changes affecting children's data, seek parental consent where required.

12. Contact Us

Privacy questions or requests? Email kidvibers.help@outlook.com.
