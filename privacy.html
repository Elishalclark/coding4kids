Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 23, 2026

KidVibers.com is built for children, so protecting privacy is at the heart of what we do. KidVibers is owned and operated by Elisha Clark ("KidVibers", "we", "us", "our"). This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the rights parents have. We follow the spirit of children's privacy laws like COPPA (the U.S. Children's Online Privacy Protection Act).

1. Information We Collect

We collect as little as possible. For a child account, that is:

  • A first name (or nickname) and a chosen username;
  • A password (stored only as a secure, hashed value - never in plain text);
  • An age range (e.g. 8-10);
  • A parent's email address (to connect a parent and send account messages);
  • Learning data: lessons completed, quiz/boss-battle results, level, streak, tokens, and avatar choices;
  • Content the child creates: code projects they save in the Playground, and any projects or comments they choose to share in the community Gallery;
  • Limited technical data: a login token (kept in your browser) and, for security and anti-abuse only, the IP address of requests (handled by our network provider). We do not build advertising profiles.

For a parent, teacher, school, or district account, we collect a name, username, password (hashed), an email address, and (for teachers, schools, and districts) a school, classroom, or organization name, plus any custom branding (display name and logo) a district chooses to add. If a kid joins a classroom, we record which teacher's class code they used so the teacher can see their own students.

Sign in with Google (grown-ups only): if a parent or teacher chooses "Continue with Google," Google sends us only your name and email address to create or sign in to your account. We don't get your Google password or anything else, and Google's button is meant for adults - kids sign in with a username and password.

2. How We Use Information

  • To run the learning experience and save progress so kids can continue where they left off;
  • To award tokens and let kids customize their avatar;
  • To power the community Gallery - letting kids share, run, like, and comment on projects;
  • To connect a parent to their child's account and send account emails (parental-consent links, a welcome message, password resets, upgrade requests a child sends a parent, and notices from an administrator);
  • To keep the platform safe - moderation, anti-abuse/rate-limiting, and security.
🎨 About the Gallery: projects and comments a child chooses to share are visible to other signed-in users and show only a first name. Sharing is optional, requires the parental-consent gate to be cleared, and everything is moderated - comments are filtered automatically and the team can remove any content or suspend an account. There is still no private messaging.
🤖 About the AI buddy ("Byte"): Byte runs entirely on our own servers and uses built-in, age-appropriate coding answers. When a child types a question, we use it only to generate Byte's reply right then - we do not send children's chats to any outside AI company, we do not use them for advertising or to train AI models, and we do not store the conversation text. We keep only a simple daily count of how many AI chats were used, to apply fair-use limits.

3. What We Never Do

🚫 We do not show ads, we do not sell or rent personal information, we do not use children's data for behavioral advertising, and we do not send children's AI chats to outside AI companies or use them to train AI. There is no private messaging between users.

4. Children & COPPA

Because KidVibers is intended for children, we require parental consent before any kid can use the account - not just children under 13, but every kid, to be extra safe. When a kid signs up, the account is held in a "pending" state and the child cannot play, do lessons, or use any feature until a parent/guardian approves it - by signing in with Google (which verifies an adult's email) and confirming they are the child's parent or guardian, by creating and linking their own Family account, or by confirming the consent link we email to the parent and then completing a short parent verification step: the parent enters their full legal name, the last 4 digits of a payment card, and confirms they are the child's parent or guardian and over 18. We never charge the card and only keep those last 4 digits - we never collect or store the full card number, expiry, or security code. This extra step helps make sure a grown-up - not the child - is giving consent. For classroom use, a teacher or school may provide consent on parents' behalf for educational purposes. We record each consent (who approved, how, and when) and collect only the information needed to provide the learning service.

A parent or guardian can review, download, or delete their child's data, sign the child out, or withdraw consent at any time from their dashboard.

5. Parent & Guardian Rights

  • Review: see your family's plan and your child's progress from the Family Dashboard.
  • Control: add kids to your family and sign them out of their devices.
  • Correct or delete: contact us to update or delete your child's information.
  • Withdraw consent: you may close the account, which removes it from active use.

6. How Information Is Shared

We don't sell or rent personal information. We share it only with a small number of trusted service providers that help us operate KidVibers, and only as needed to provide the service - or when required by law or to keep someone safe:

  • Hosting, network & delivery (Cloudflare): KidVibers runs on Cloudflare Workers and stores account data in Cloudflare's D1 database; Cloudflare also routes traffic, provides HTTPS, and helps block abuse;
  • Email delivery (Resend / Amazon SES): sends account emails such as parental-consent links, welcome messages, password resets, and notices;
  • Google Sign-In: if a parent/teacher chooses "Continue with Google," Google verifies their identity and shares their name and email with us (grown-ups only);
  • Payment processing: if and when paid plans go live, card payments will be handled entirely by a PCI-compliant processor (e.g. Stripe) - we never see or store card numbers. (Paid plans are not currently being charged.)

8. Third-Party Services

KidVibers uses a few third-party services, chosen to keep data collection minimal:

  • Cloudflare - hosting (Workers + D1 database), network, HTTPS, and abuse protection;
  • Resend (Amazon SES) - delivers our account emails;
  • Google Sign-In - optional "Continue with Google" for parents & teachers; we receive only name and email;
  • QR image service - generates the parent-invite QR code; the only data sent is the invite link;
  • jsDelivr (CDN) - serves the in-browser Python engine (Skulpt) for the Playground; code kids write runs locally in their browser and is not sent to a server.

9. How We Protect Information

Passwords are stored using strong one-way hashing (PBKDF2 with a unique salt) - we can't see them. Traffic is served over HTTPS, and we send security headers on every response (HSTS to force HTTPS, anti-clickjacking, MIME-sniffing protection, and a strict referrer policy). Logins are rate-limited, sign-ups are throttled to prevent abuse, and display names/comments are sanitized to prevent code injection. Assessment answer keys stay on the server and are never sent to the browser. Login sessions are token-based and can be revoked (for example, when a parent signs a kid out, or an account is suspended).

10. Keeping & Deleting Data

We keep account information and progress so kids don't lose their work - registered accounts are retained until deletion is requested. To delete an account and its associated data, a parent or account owner can contact us at the address below.
