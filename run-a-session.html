Help Run a KidVibers Session

A quick one-page guide for helping kids code at a library or school session.

🌟 The vibe (read this first)

KidVibers is vibe coding — kids build real websites, cards, and pages by telling their buddy Byte what they want. It's not hardcore typing-code, and Byte is not AI. The goal is simple: kids have fun making cool stuff and feel like creators. Keep it light and celebrate what they build!

✅ Before kids arrive

  1. Log in and open the dashboard.
  2. Click Start a Live Session — this gives you a join code and a QR flyer.
  3. Print or show the QR flyer so kids can scan and join. (Tablets/laptops ready and charged!)
  4. When enough kids are in, hit Lock Session so no one else can join.

🙋 How kids join

  • They scan the QR code (or go to kidvibers.com/live and type the code).
  • They pick a nickname — no account or email needed.
  • They're in! They can jump straight into Vibe Studio or a lesson.

💬 What kids always ask

  • "What do I build?" → Anything! A page about their pet, their favorite team, a birthday card. Point them to the template buttons.
  • "How do I make it?" → Just tell Byte what you want, then edit the words and pictures.
  • "How do I save it?" → Hit the 💾 Save my project button.
  • "I'm stuck!" → Have them pick a template to start, or grab you.

⚠️ Keep it safe

  • Best practice: have 2 adults present whenever possible — it protects both the kids and the volunteers running the session.
  • Stay where you can see the screens. Kids first!
  • Don't share your own login or passwords with anyone.
  • If a kid seems upset or writes something worrying, tell the lead adult — the system also flags it automatically.
  • Only the lead runs the account settings. When in doubt, ask.

🏁 Wrapping up

  1. Give a 5-minute warning: "Time to save your project!"
  2. Let a few kids show off what they made — it's the best part. 🎉
  3. Hand out flyers so families can keep playing at home.
  4. Click End Session on the dashboard.

💡 Golden rule

You don't need to know how to code. Your job is to keep kids excited, help them get un-stuck, and cheer for what they build. If something breaks or you're unsure — just ask the lead. Have fun! 🚀
