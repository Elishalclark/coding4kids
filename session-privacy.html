🔒 What we collect during a Live Session

A plain-language, one-page summary for parents and library/school staff — no legal jargon. For the full policy, see our Privacy Policy.

✅ What we ask for

Just a nickname. No email, no password, no birthdate, no photo — unless the host has separately collected photo/video consent for the in-person event.

🗑️ How long it's kept

The account is deleted automatically the next day — including anything they built — unless a grown-up chooses to save it.

💾 "Save my account"

If a kid wants to keep what they made, the session-ended screen lets a parent/guardian enter their email and set a real password on the spot.

👀 Who can see it

Only the adult running the session (roster + a live feed of what's being built) and KidVibers staff for safety moderation. We never sell or share kid data.

Why this is allowed without a parent's email up front

Under COPPA, a school or library acting in a "school official" capacity can consent on behalf of parents for a strictly educational, in-program activity. That's the basis for a drop-in Live Session: the host institution is providing consent for the duration of the program, which is why kids can jump in with just a nickname instead of a full sign-up.

What gets deleted

  • Their account (nickname, login)
  • Anything they built or saved during the session
  • Lesson progress and quiz results from the session
  • Any chat messages exchanged with Byte (KidVibers' buddy — not a real AI, and not a real person)

Questions?

Reach us any time at privacy@kidvibers.com. Library/school staff: our full School Privacy & DPA page has retention tables, sub-processors, and a signable Data Processing Agreement.
