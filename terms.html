1. Who Can Use KidVibers

KidVibers is designed for children, generally ages 6-16, learning to code. Because our users include children, a parent or guardian must be involved in creating and managing a child's account, and consents to these Terms on the child's behalf. Every kid account requires a parent or guardian to approve it before the child can play, do lessons, or use any feature - this applies to all kids, not only those under 13.

2. Accounts

  • Keep your username and password private. You are responsible for activity on your account.
  • Provide accurate information when registering (for a child: a first name, a username, an age range, and a parent's email).
  • Kids cannot sign themselves out - a connected parent or an administrator can sign a child out at any time.
  • Parents and teachers may optionally sign in with Google ("Continue with Google"); this is for grown-ups, and kids use a username and password.
  • We keep registered accounts and learning progress so kids can pick up where they left off.

3. Parents, Families, Teachers & Schools

When a child registers, we generate a secure invite (a QR code and email link) so a parent can create their own account and connect it to the child's. Parents on a Family plan can add kids, view their family's plan and progress, receive messages, and sign their kids out. A parent only ever sees their own family's information.

Teachers and schools can create a classroom or school account and add students one at a time or in bulk via a class roster upload (CSV), up to their plan's student limit. Each teacher account has a unique class code; a kid can enter that code to join the teacher's classroom. Teachers, schools, and districts can also set School Hours (when students may log in), assign lessons with due dates, and download progress reports. For classroom use, the teacher or school provides consent for educational use on parents' behalf (consistent with FTC school guidance), and is responsible for having the right to enroll those students. Teachers/schools see only their own students.

Districts and libraries can use a District account to manage many classrooms or sites under one roof - set custom branding (name and logo), manage the number of student seats they signed up for, and suspend, edit, or remove the student accounts they oversee. A District/School administrator is responsible for the accounts they create and manage, and sees only the accounts within their own organization.

4. Fair Play & the No-Cheat Policy

KidVibers is about real learning. When using lessons and assessments (including unit "boss battle" tests), you agree not to:

  • Share or copy test answers with anyone;
  • Exploit bugs or glitches to gain an advantage;
  • Create extra accounts to bypass limits or restrictions;
  • Use unauthorized tools or outside help during an assessment.

Breaking these rules may lead to a warning, having a test result invalidated, an account review, or a temporary suspension.

5. Plans, Trials & Billing

KidVibers offers a Free tier and a short free Trial, paid Pro and Family plans for kids and parents, and Teacher, School, and District plans for educators, schools, libraries, and districts. Paid plans unlock additional features (such as the AI buddy, the full curriculum, and adding more students). Trials and some features are limited. Plans can be billed monthly or annually; annual subscribers get a reminder email about a week before renewal. Current plans, prices, and limits are shown on our Pricing page and may change.

The AI buddy ("Byte"). Some plans include an in-app AI buddy that gives friendly coding hints and explanations, helps with math, defines coding terms, and gives feedback in the Prompt Lab. Byte is a learning aid and can be incomplete or wrong - it is not a substitute for a teacher, and it should not be used for anything outside of learning to code. Don't try to misuse it or get it to produce unsafe or inappropriate content. AI features may be limited per day and may change.

Referral rewards. A kid can share their personal invite code or link with a friend. When a friend signs up using it, both kids receive bonus tokens and a limited number of free days of Pro-level access. Referral rewards are promotional, may change or end at any time, and have no cash value.

Billing status: paid plans are not currently being charged while we finish setting up payments - no one is billed yet, and visiting checkout does not change your plan. When billing goes live, card payments will be handled by a PCI-compliant processor (e.g. Stripe); the account owner is responsible for any charges and may cancel at any time, with access continuing through the current billing period.

Tokens and items are virtual, have no real-world cash value, cannot be exchanged for money, and are non-transferable and non-refundable. We may adjust token rewards, prices, or the catalog at any time.

7. Your Projects & Content

Kids keep ownership of the projects and creations they make in Vibe Studio and the lessons. Projects are private to your account and family/classroom - there is no public gallery or sharing feature on KidVibers, and no private messaging between users. A parent, teacher, or administrator connected to the account can view or download a kid's projects and data at any time.

8. Reporting a Problem

Every kid dashboard has an always-available "Something's wrong?" button so a kid can quickly flag a bug, unkind behavior, something that scared or confused them, or anything else - it immediately notifies the connected parent/guardian (and our team) so a grown-up can check in. This is separate from, and does not replace, talking to a trusted adult directly. Teachers and schools can also flag a student's account to us for review, and we may suspend or remove an account that puts other users at risk (see Section 9).
